Avial

Description

Avial is a traditional South Indian dish made with mixed vegetables, coconut, yogurt, and aromatic spices. It is a popular dish in Kerala and Tamil Nadu, typically served with rice or adai (lentil dosa).

The vegetables are cooked in a coconut-yogurt gravy, giving the dish a creamy texture and a burst of flavors.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrot, beans, potato, pumpkin, drumstick, etc.)
  • 1/2 cup grated coconut
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 green chili
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • 1 tablespoon coconut oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing

Steps

  1. Cut the vegetables into equal-sized pieces and cook them until soft.
  2. Grind coconut, cumin seeds, and green chili to a smooth paste.
  3. Add the coconut paste, turmeric powder, and salt to the cooked vegetables.
  4. Simmer for a few minutes, then add yogurt and mix well.
  5. Heat coconut oil in a pan, add mustard seeds and curry leaves, and pour this tempering over the avial.
  6. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or adai.
