Misal Pav

Description

Misal Pav is a popular Maharashtrian street food made with sprouted moth beans, onions, tomatoes, and aromatic spices. It is typically served with pav (bread rolls) and garnished with farsan (crispy toppings) and lemon wedges.

The dish has multiple layers of flavors and textures, making it a favorite among spice lovers.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup sprouted moth beans (matki)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 large tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1/2 inch ginger, finely chopped
  • 3-4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnishing
  • Farsan (crispy toppings) for garnishing
  • Lemon wedges for serving

Steps

  1. Pressure cook the sprouted moth beans with water until soft.
  2. Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add onions, garlic, ginger, and saut√© until onions turn golden.
  4. Add tomatoes, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until tomatoes turn mushy.
  5. Mix in the cooked moth beans and cook for a few minutes.
  6. Stir in garam masala and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  7. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and farsan.
  8. Serve hot with pav and lemon wedges.
